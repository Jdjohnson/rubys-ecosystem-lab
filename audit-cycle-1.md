## Observations
- The app’s base tone is warm and kid-friendly, and the large-card layout is generally stronger than the average educational web app.
- The biggest pattern problem is “the UI does the thinking for the learner.” Icons, section labels, role colors, and fallback error copy frequently reveal the answer instead of setting up a clean reasoning loop.
- The biggest UX risk after that is invisible rules: several screens expect the learner to infer how much exploration is “enough,” when to continue, or why a button appeared.
- The science foundation is mostly solid, but a few contradictions and oversimplifications are strong enough to erode trust because the whole app is teaching categorical rules.
- iPad support is partly there via safe-area padding and generally large cards, but the current runtime hydration errors, disabled zoom, SVG touch sizes, and viewport assumptions still make the experience feel less robust than the visual design suggests.
